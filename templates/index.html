Best Practices

1

Quiet Environment

High Fidelity Mode disables noise suppression. You must be in a silent room. Fan noise or AC will confuse the analyzer.

2

Mic Position

Don't breathe directly into the mic to prevent "popping". If using a headset, place it to the side of your mouth.

3

Clear Articulation

Articulate ending consonants distinctively (e.g., "cat", "book"). The system stops automatically when you stop speaking.

4

Avoid Extra Sounds

Do not smack lips or click your tongue before speaking. This triggers the recording too early.
